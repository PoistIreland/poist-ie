Irish Language Screening

This screening gives you an indicative Irish language proficiency level for your Language Passport. Employers use it to understand your ability before making contact.

About this screening
This is a CEFR-referenced screening — it is designed around the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages guidelines, but it is not a formal certification. Your result is an indicative level based on your performance across four areas. Formal qualifications such as a TEG certificate or your Leaving Certificate grade are noted separately on your passport.
What's involved
🎧
Listening — 3 questions
Listen to a short clip in Irish, then answer questions about what you heard. You can listen twice. Auto-scored. (~5 min)
📝
Reading (gap-fill) — 4 blanks
Read an Irish passage and select the correct word for each gap. Auto-scored. (~5 min)
✍️
Writing — 1 short task
Write a short piece in Irish. Assessed automatically using AI — scored on vocabulary, grammar and structure. (~7 min)
🎤
Spoken sample — read a short passage aloud
Record yourself reading an Irish passage. This clip is stored on your Language Passport so employers can hear your spoken Irish before deciding to contact you. (~3 min)
💡 Spoken interaction (conversation) is not part of this screening — that is best assessed at interview stage where you can speak naturally with an Irish-speaking interviewer.
