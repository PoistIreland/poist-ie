The CEFR Guide for Irish Speakers

The Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) is the standard poist.ie uses to verify and match Irish language proficiency. Here's what each level means.

Why CEFR matters on poist.ie

Standard CVs use vague terms like "some Irish" or "conversational". CEFR eliminates that ambiguity. Every candidate on poist.ie selects their verified CEFR level — so employers know exactly what they're hiring, and candidates know exactly what a role requires.

The Six CEFR Levels

A1
Beginner
Can understand and use very basic Irish phrases. Can introduce themselves and answer simple questions about personal details.
Typical roles: Admin support with basic Irish requirements, some community-facing roles.
A2
Elementary
Can communicate in simple, routine tasks. Understands frequently used expressions related to work, family and local area.
Typical roles: Customer service, reception roles with Irish-speaking public.
B1
Intermediate
Can deal with most situations likely to arise when working in an Irish-language environment. Can produce simple connected text on familiar topics.
Typical roles: Local authority staff, HSE community workers, education support.
B2
Upper-Intermediate
Can understand the main ideas of complex text. Can interact with a degree of fluency with native speakers without strain for either party.
Typical roles: Irish Language Officers, communications roles, policy, bilingual teams. Most common requirement on poist.ie.
C1
Advanced
Can express ideas fluently and spontaneously. Can use Irish effectively for social, academic and professional purposes.
Typical roles: Senior communications, TG4 / media production, academic positions, Gaeltacht-facing management.
C2
Proficient / Native
Can understand virtually everything heard or read. Can express themselves spontaneously, fluently and precisely — even in complex situations.
Typical roles: Native Irish speaker roles, Gaeltacht-based positions, Irish language translation, senior Gaeltacht authority roles.
